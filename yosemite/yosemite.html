Yosemite Trip - July 2017

Part 1: The Part Before We Actually Get To Yosemite

A little while before: Inception

I planned on being in San Francisco from July 25 to the 30th but somehow, a month or two prior to my trip I got the idea that I wanted to hike to the top of Half Dome. I didn't know shit about Yosemite, much less hiking Half Dome but I think it came to me in a suggested Youtube video while watching rock climbing videos.

I suggested it to Phil. The weekend I would be there was Anne's birthday and he thought it would make for a great birthday surprise trip. Awesome!

A day later I started doing some research. Shit... all of the prime camping in Yosemite Valley was booked, not to mention the permits to use the cables on Half Dome! Fortunately, the surprise had ended: Anne knew about the idea and was not only on board, she spearheaded the plan herself.

Before I knew it, Anne had already found an available campsite on Hipcamp (in SF speak: "think Uber for campsites") and had sent out an email to all of her and Phil's friends which I had remembered as more of a 'hey would anyone be interested in this crazy plan' email, but in re-reading reads more like a 'here are the plans; who's in' email. I guess when you live in San Francisco going to Yosemite isn't really a big deal.

Friends confirm, campsite is booked, woo wap da bam: we're going to Yosemite!

A short while before (aka Thursday): Getting A Permit

We're booked for a campsite, but we don't have a permit to get on the cables that guide and support you to the top of Half Dome. All the preseason permits were awarded months ago, so our only opportunity at getting permits is in the daily lottery. Applications for the daily lottery are due two days prior to the permit date, so today is our day if we want permits for Saturday.

I'm a lucky idiot. For some reason, I forget to apply for the permit in time today. As luck would have it though, Anne, the only permit applicant in the party, did not forget to apply and beat the odds by winning the lottery for six permits! We learned this late Thursday night.

We got pretty lucky. The graph below displays "Daily Lottery Success Rate per Day" for 2016 (source). You can see that last year, the average Saturday success rate for Saturdays in July was somewhere around 10% (the 2nd, 9th, 16th, 23rd, and 30th are all Saturdays).

If we were serious, everyone in the party would have applied. That would have costed us some money -- there's a $10 fee per application -- but it would have practically ensured we would be able to hike the cables.

Whatever. Fortunately we don't have to think about what could've been because it was. Thanks birthday luck!

The Day of the Drive

"Anything with rhubarb"

Our goal is to set out at 2pm. I receive a text from Phil around 1pm:

"Henry ! What are the chances you can run to a pie store..."

Ayeee! I'm still on the clock at work, and we haven't really even started packing the car to leave yet. Not to mention: how am I going to explain me bailing out to go to a pie store and hour before we're supposed to be getting on the road?

My USB-C Experience the Day Prior

Birthday luck does indeed strike twice, though. I had bought a USB-C cable from Walgreens for $25 dollars earlier in the week. That's not the birthday luck... I had to buy it because I left my charger at Jordan's house in Chicago. Mike has an Android so I was able to use his charger in LA, but both Anne and Phil are iPhone people so the day after I got into SF I went to Walgreens and combed through every cable they had.

They had a million fucking Micro USB cables for like $5, but the only two USB-C offerings they had were a male USB-C to male USB-A cable (the kind I wanted) and a male USB-C to female USB-A (something for people who bought the new MacBooks with only USB-C but still want to plug in their shitty Dell keyboard that uses USB-A or something).

The regular cable was $25. The weird, female-A cable was on sale and had been marked down from something outrageous, like $25, to something reasonable, like $8 -- I guess there aren't that many people with 2016 MacBooks still using Dell keyboards.

I had turned off my phone at about 8% battery. I turned it on and went to the Amazon app and then the Amazon Now app (Why tf is there a separate app for Now?) to look for alternatives. This search turned up fruitless.

Then I tried to think of any possible way I could use the female USB-A cable. There are very few ways to use that cable to charge a phone.

I brought the $25 cable up to the register.

"Is this really the only USB-C cable you have?"

"All the USB cables that we have are in that section [that you just pored through for 10 minutes]"

"This cable should not be this expensive. This is friggin outrageous! The Micro-USB cables are like a quarter of the price."

I knew full well that this dude had no control over the price of the items in the store. I wondered how he could afford to live in this city with, what I assumed, was not a very high paying job. Maybe the reason the price of this cable is so high is because Walgreens has to pay their cashiers three times as much here as they do in Minneapolis?

"Well, this one is a Belkin. Those other ones will break sooner than this one.", he replied.

"Well why the hell don't you carry any of the shitty USB-C cables that break sooner? I only need this cable for the weekend before I'm back in Minneapolis where I have like four USB-C cables that I bought off Amazon for like eight dollars -- I mean I really only needed one but the kind I wanted is only like three inches long and they only sell those in five packs."

I didn't actually say that. I bought the twenty-five dollar USB cable.

"What's the return policy on this?"

"You can return it to any Walgreens location within 30 days of your purchase"

"You can bet I'll be doing that!"

He smiled. This whole exchange wasn't tense at all. He knew I was just messing with him.

The Comcast Dude

Anne gets a call on her phone from the Comcast dude; he's here to do something with the internet. Just the distraction I need!

Guy comes in. I grab that USB-C cable box and show it to her and say "Yo I'mma go return this at the Walgreens because it was really expensive and yeah I'll be back!"

When I leave Anne and this dude are having a conversation about his Star Wars ringtone or something. I would later learn that this Comcast dude was a major weirdo. Sorry for leaving you solo with that guy, Anne. Had I known he was a creep I wouldn't have left, but I was really distracted with coming up with an excuse to bail.

The Pie Store

I ride Phil's new Surly Cross Check to the pie store. He got that bike when he moved out there -- it's frickin badass.

"Do you guys have a rhubarb pie?"

I thought I was trying to get a whole pie. On the ride over I had had three thoughts:

First: Are they even gonna have a whole rhubarb pie ready? I feel like we should have called this in like 8 hours ago.

Second: How tf am I going to bring a whole pie back? There's no way a whole pie will sit facing up in this backpack I've got on!

Third: Wtf is rhubarb?

Fortunately, this place had mini pies, and a big variety!

"I'll have four of these strawberry rhubarb ones, three pear ones, two of these blueberry ones, and... hmmm... which is the most popular one that I haven't already selected?"

"People seem to like these chocolate creme brûlée one's. Or else there's the vanilla one."

"Okay, I'll get one of each."

He rings me up. I say, "Hey... what do you think about giving me these ten pies for, like, the price of eight?"

He agrees. I'm so flattered by the fact that that worked that I say "keep the change" and give him practically all the money I saved. That makes me feel dumb on the ride home.

Don't Crush the Pies!

When I get back to the apartment, I realize I don't have the keys and I'm upset with myself because now Anne is gonna have to come down and let me in and then the surprise will be over. Birthday luck strikes again -- they have a buzzer! I stash the pie boxes at the bottom of the stairs, run up to the apartment, get the keys to the Prius, and bring my backpack down, grabbing the pie boxes on the way.

Priuses aren't very big. The trunk is almost full with the cooler and firewood and tents. In the back, Anne and Phil's bags occupy the middle seat. I decide that under the passenger seat is the best place to put the pies. I go upstairs. Anne is ready so we hit the road to go pick up Miles.

When we arrive at Miles's, Anne gives him a call that goes unanswered but soon after he emerges from the building and places one of his bags in the back seat behind the driver's side before running back in to grab some more stuff. It comes to my realization that this means that he will be sitting on the passenger side with his feet at the pies. I become worried that he'll either crush the box or pull it out, thus ruining the surprise.

"What's Miles's number???", I frantically text Phil. He responds with the number and I'm able to send a string of messages to Miles:

"Hey miles it's Henry in annes car outside" - 2:46:25 pm
"Be careful of the pies at your feet on my sidw" - 2:46:26 pm
"But it's a secret!!!" - 2:46:27 pm

When he came out of the apartment it was so soon after the messages had been sent that I was unsure if he had even seen them. I got out, offered to help him out, and shut the door. Discretely I try to convey the information.

"There's a bag at your feet! Don't crush it and don't bring it up! "

Anne opens her door and gets out. "Do you guys know each other?" she asks.

"I know", Miles replies and an immense feeling of relief comes over me.

"No, we don't know each other", I reply as we get into the car and head into San Francisco traffic.

Finally on the Road

After picking up Miles, we head to REI where I got a green Nalgene because I forgot my last one in LA, two orange Black Diamond quickdraws, two 24" slings (one red and one green), and a really shitty small carabiner for my water bottle. After that we think about going to the hardware store because we really need gloves for the cables but the traffic is so bad that we instead head straight to Phil's work which is right next to some brewery so Miles goes there to fill a growler that he happened to bring and during that time Phil comes out and I offer him the front seat so I can sit with the pies and Anne gets out of the car for a moment so I show Phil the pies and we share the vanilla one and then Anne and Miles come back to the car and get in and we finally hit the road.

The drive wasn't bad.

Somewhere after Oakland we stopped at an In-N-Out. Before that, Phil had mentioned that he was feeling two burgers so I followed suit. I figured that since it was In-N-Out I had to get something weird about my burger so I asked for "the cheese you put on the cheese fries", to which the woman behind the counter replied "we put the same cheese on the cheese fries that we put on the burgers" and I said "oh" and she said "do you want extra cheese?" and I was like "uhh, yeah, let's do that for one of the burgers". I'm glad I didn't do it on both. 'Extra Cheese' at In-N-Out happens to mean 'an inch thick stack of cheese slices' and I was disappointed. I was further disappointed when I realized that when Phil meant two burgers he actually meant two double burgers.

I feel like I'm always disappointed when I go to In-N-Out. I know it's heresy, but imho, In-N-Out fucking sucks. I never feel like I order the right thing there and I'm starting to think that's because there really isn't a right thing to order there because all of their sandwiches are subpar but people think they're good because you can ask for an "extra toasted bun" or get it "animal style".

Anyways, after In-N-Out, both Miles and Phil passed tf out, I stayed awake, we pressed the button that says "Faster route available", we missed the cut to get us back from the alternate route, we drove through a bunch of different types of trees (driving in California is way more interesting than driving through the Midwest because there is more than just corn), we saw some hills, I took over driving at one point, we got to the campsite, and I had to gun the Prius to get it up the really steep, gravel hill to our campsite. After we set up camp, I kicked my foot on a tent stake, the rest of the party arrived, and we went to bed.

Part 2: The Part Where We're Actually At Yosemite

The Aux Cord

The next morning, on Saturday, we woke up at 3:30am and “made” breakfast. For me that meant opening a Clif Bar.

After everyone was up and fed, we begin our drive to Yosemite. From our Hipcamp, it wasn't very far into Yosemite National Park itself, maybe about 15 minutes, but then it took another 45 minutes just to reach the parking lot in Yosemite Valley. The drive in wasn't the most comfortable. For one, everyone had only slept like 5 hours, but to add further discomfort, the aux cable wasn't working for some reason. Phil was sitting in passenger and was a really good sport about the number of requests we gave him in an effort to get some music playing:

"Okay, press the aux button again."
"Try wiggling the cord a little bit more."
"What if we tried using the end of the cord that's plugged into the car as the phone end?"
"Anne, at the next stop, we should turn off the car and turn it back on. Maybe that will help."

None of those suggestions worked, so I held my phone speaker side up in the middle console so everyone could just barely hear it. The song Hug of Thunder had just come out, but from my phone speakers the thunder just wasn't there.

The Start of the Hike

When we got to the parking lot, it was about 5:15am. The parking lot was actually pretty active with a lot of people standing outside of their cars looking tired and getting ready to hike. While you’re driving into the valley, you can see the sides of the valley, but the enormity of the walls doesn’t really become apparent until you get out of the car and have to tilt your head up to see the top of the wall.

After tilting our heads up, making sure we had everything we needed, and making sure we had everything we needed again, we started walking towards the trail. At the edge of the parking lot there’s a sign that says something to the extent of “yo there are bears so don’t leave food visibly in your car or else we’ll give you a ticket”. We had brought all of our food from the camp site and it was in the back of the Prius so Anne and Mikey ran back to the car and moved it from the hatchback to the trunk of Mikey’s Camry so the park rangers wouldn’t be able to see the coolers. We wondered why it mattered if the food was visible. Did bears understand what a cooler is? We returned to the joke when we were further up the trail and we imagined looking down at the parking lot, appalled by the sight of a bear peeling off the roof of the Prius like a can of sardines so he could access the turkey sausage and PBR inside the red and white cooler that he could see through the hatchback window.

The hike began smoothly. At the beginning you walk along a paved trail. After that part you cross a bridge over a stream where, on the other side, is the last spot to refill your water bottles. When we got there, we were surprised and upset to find out that that water fountain was out of order. Why the fuck they didn’t mention that way before here we didn’t know.

The sign at that fountain said something like “ayee, if you wanna get to the top of Half Dome we recommend having at least like a half a gallon of water, but a whole gallon probably isn’t a bad idea” with pictures of the number of Nalgene water bottles you should have. I think the recommended number was like six. Unfortunately, I only had one half full Nalgene, but I trusted Anne when she said that there is access to the stream further up the hike, and we proceded.

After that bridge you walk along the stream and eventually to a big bunch of huge, granite stairs next to a waterfall called Vernal Falls.

At the top of the Falls we regrouped by the water access point. The group mentioned something called Giardia that is in the water and will make you sick. “There’s giardiniera in the water?” I asked, bringing on the first of many longings I would get for an Italian beef sandwich. We cleaned the water of giardiniera using a bottle that you fill up with dirty water and put a filter/lid on top. The water from the stream was frickin delicious and was super cold too.

The hike continued going through flat lands and forests and switch-backs. Certain parts of the forest had obvious signs of forest fire. Fallen trees laid among still-standing trees with burnt-out bases. I wondered if the trees that were still standing despite their injuries would die or survive.
